Entering the American Job Market as an International Student

In the video, Gina explains her journey as an international student that is trying to find work in America. She goes through the most typical process that most international students take, which is obtaining the 1 year of OPT that allows students to work for an year without work sponsorship after graduation. For STEM majors like Gina, this can be extended up to 3 years. However, once again, when the OPT expires, all international students must make a choice between visa sponsorship or leaving the country.

Gina Jang

Gina (Yejin) Jang is an international student that is graduating this semester from UNC Chapel Hill. This is her fifth year in America with a F-1 visa. Although she majors in computer science and mathematics both of which are supposed to guarantee success in the job market, she is struggling to find a job. Even though she nows exactly why, there is nothing much she can do about it.

As explained in the video, as an international student that is currently in America with a F-1 visa, Gina must find a employer that will sponsor H-1B visa. However, not many employers are willing to do so as the cost of sponsoring the visa is almost equal to that of hiring another worker. Therefore, although Gina hopes to remain in America, if the situation does not work out, she is required to leave the company in an year.
