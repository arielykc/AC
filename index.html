F-1 Visa Work Authorization in America

The complicated process that international students must go through in order to work in America after graduation

✳

Key Terms of Work Authorization

If you originally came to America with an F-1 student visa, work authorization is not given, meaning you must go through a separate process in order to be able to work. With the process being complicated and rigid, slight mistake could get you deported which is why you must be very knowledgable of the entire process.

✳

F-1 Visa

An F-1 visa is a non-immigrant student visa that allows international students to study full-time in the United States.

*No work authorization granted

✳

Curricular Practical Training (CPT)

CPT involves working an internship or gaining training that’s required by your major to graduate.

*Part-time work authorization

✳

Optional Practical Training (OPT)

OPT, is a program that gives international students with F-1 immigration status the opportunity to work in the US for a limited time.

*1-3 years of work authorization

✳

H-1B

The H-1B visa is a temporary work visa that allows employers to hire foreign nationals for specialty occupations in the United States.

✳

Green Card

Green Card (officially known as a Permanent Resident Card allows you to live and work permanently in the United States.

*Full work authorization

✳

Citizenship

Citizenship is a membership and allegiance to a sovereign state. Full rights is granted.

*Full work authorization

✳
